- Question: How is HIV passed on?
- Answer: HIV can be passed on when infected bodily fluid, such as blood or semen, is passed into an uninfected person.

- Question: Which bodily fluids are mentioned as able to carry HIV?
- Answer: Blood and semen are mentioned as bodily fluids that can carry HIV.

- Question: What is semen?
- Answer: Semen is the liquid released from a man's penis during sex which carries sperm and can be infected with HIV if the person is HIV positive.

- Question: Can HIV be transmitted during unprotected sex?
- Answer: Yes — for example, when two people have sex without using a condom and one partner is already infected.

- Question: Can sharing needles transmit HIV?
- Answer: Yes — HIV can be transmitted between drug users who inject and share needles.

- Question: Can HIV be transmitted by coughing or sneezing?
- Answer: No — HIV cannot be transmitted by coughing or sneezing.

- Question: Can HIV be passed by kissing?
- Answer: No — kissing is not listed as a means of transmitting HIV.

- Question: Can HIV be transmitted through sharing a toilet seat or using a swimming pool?
- Answer: No — HIV cannot be transmitted by sharing a toilet seat or through swimming pools.

- Question: Can sweat or tears transmit HIV?
- Answer: No — sweat and tears are not routes of HIV transmission.

- Question: What does it mean if someone's semen is infected with HIV?
- Answer: It means the person is HIV positive or carrying the AIDS virus, and their semen can carry the virus and potentially infect others.
