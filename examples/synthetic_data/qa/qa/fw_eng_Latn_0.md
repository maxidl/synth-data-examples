- Question: What central themes are said to be at the heart of Jane Austen’s books?
- Answer: Freedom and independence—independence of thought and the freedom to choose.

- Question: How did Elizabeth Bennet demonstrate independence in her marital decisions?
- Answer: She refused Mr. Collins’s offer of marriage and initially refused Mr. Darcy’s proposal, actions that showed an uncommon independence for heroines of the day.

- Question: In what way did Elizabeth ultimately show freedom when accepting Mr. Darcy?
- Answer: She accepted him in direct defiance of Lady Catherine and despite knowing her father would disapprove, demonstrating personal freedom.

- Question: Which character is persuaded to refuse Captain Wentworth, and who persuades her?
- Answer: Anne Elliot is persuaded to refuse Captain Wentworth at the insistence of Lady Russell.

- Question: How does the text describe Jane Austen’s attitude toward the limitations placed on women?
- Answer: Her writing is infused with how she wanted life to be and she “screams” her outrage at the limitations for women, particularly in Emma.

- Question: What point does Jane Fairfax make about the “governess-trade” in her exchange with Mrs. Elton?
- Answer: Jane contrasts the governess-trade with the slave trade, implying that while different in guilt, governesses may suffer greater misery as they are effectively selling their intellect and labor.

- Question: How does Emma react when Mrs. Weston reveals Frank Churchill’s secret engagement to Jane Fairfax?
- Answer: Emma is shocked and exclaims that Jane being on the point of going as a governess is a “horrible indelicacy,” unable to conceive such a measure.

- Question: What historical coincidence involving John Adams and a birth is noted, and why is it significant?
- Answer: John Adams left his farm in mid-December 1775 for the Continental Congress to consider independence, and around that time a baby girl (Jane Austen) was born in England; the coincidence links two figures associated with declarations of independence—political and literary.

- Question: How is Jane Austen’s influence compared to Thomas Jefferson’s?
- Answer: Austen’s impact is presented as no less important though more subtle; Jefferson’s words instigated a political revolution, while Austen quietly promoted principles of freedom, self-regulated independence, and individual rights through her novels.

- Question: Despite being described as an avowed royalist, what Jeffersonian principle is Jane Austen said to have believed in?
- Answer: She is said to have believed in the declaration of the right to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness, and advocated independence of person and thought, rights for all, and acceptance of responsibility for those rights.
