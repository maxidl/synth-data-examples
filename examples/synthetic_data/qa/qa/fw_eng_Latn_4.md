- Question: Who wrote the piece about hunting for fallen leaves?
- Answer: Mary Catherine Ball

- Question: Where did the reporter go to gather fallen leaves?
- Answer: Into the woods

- Question: What three challenges did the reporter encounter on the way to the woods?
- Answer: Crossing a mud-ridden stream, facing flying creatures trying to get near, and enduring spiders (webmakers) spinning hair into a shiny maze

- Question: How many leaves did the reporter have analyzed, and which three tree types were identified?
- Answer: Five leaves were analyzed; they included leaves from an oak, a beech, and a sweet gum tree

- Question: List the five leaf-hunting clues given for the trees to find.
- Answer: Narrow body with pointy edges; Narrow body with pointy edges (repeated); May grow berries; Good for sap & color; 3 distinct leafs; May grow nuts
